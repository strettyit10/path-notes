💙 Congenital Heart Disease

6 diseases
VSD — Ventricular Septal Defect
Most common congenital heart defect · L→R shunt → Eisenmenger if untreated
Congenital
⚙ MOA / Pathophys
LV→RV shunt (L pressure > R) → ↑ pulmonary flow → RV volume overload. Small defect = higher velocity jet = LOUDER murmur. Eisenmenger: chronic ↑ pulmonary pressure → pulmonary HTN → R pressure exceeds L → R→L reversal → cyanosis.
🩺 Presentation / Buzzwords
Holosystolic murmur at LLSB · Failure to thrive · Tachypnea · Infant CHF
LLSB holosystolicMost common CHDEisenmenger lateSpontaneous closure
💊 Treatment / ECG
Small: observe (often closes). Large/symptomatic: surgical/catheter repair. Eisenmenger: surgery CONTRAINDICATED. ECG: LA+LV enlargement; RVH if pulmonary HTN.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
Most common congenital heart defect
Small VSD = louder murmur (high-velocity jet)
Eisenmenger: L→R reverses → late cyanosis → surgery CONTRAINDICATED
LLSB location distinguishes from MR (apex → axilla)
Holosystolic ≠ always MR. VSD = LLSB; MR = apex → axilla. Surgery after Eisenmenger is too late.
ASD — Atrial Septal Defect
Ostium secundum most common · Fixed split S2 is pathognomonic
Congenital
⚙ MOA / Pathophys
L→R atrial shunt → RA/RV volume overload → right-sided dilation. ASD equalizes atrial pressures → removes normal respiratory variation in S2 splitting → fixed split S2. Murmur = increased pulmonic flow murmur (not through defect).
🩺 Presentation / Buzzwords
Wide FIXED split S2 · Exercise intolerance · Often asymptomatic in childhood · RA/RV enlargement
Fixed split S2Down syndromeSilent shuntOstium secundum MC
💊 Treatment / ECG
Observation if small. Closure (transcatheter/surgical) if significant. ECG: RAD, incomplete RBBB (rsR' in V1), RV enlargement.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
Fixed split S2 = ASD — no other condition causes this
Murmur is a pulmonic FLOW murmur, not through the defect
Down syndrome → endocardial cushion defect (AV canal) → ASD + VSD
Paradoxical emboli: venous clot → crosses ASD → systemic embolism
Fixed split ≠ wide split. Fixed = same in inspiration/expiration = ASD. Wide split (no fixation) = RBBB.
PDA — Patent Ductus Arteriosus
Continuous machine-like murmur · Indomethacin closes · PGE opens
Congenital
⚙ MOA / Pathophys
Aorta → pulmonary artery shunt throughout cardiac cycle (systole + diastole) → continuous murmur. Aortic diastolic runoff → wide pulse pressure + bounding pulses. Indomethacin: ↓ prostaglandins → ductal smooth muscle constriction → closure.
🩺 Presentation / Buzzwords
Continuous machine-like murmur · Bounding pulses · Wide pulse pressure · Prematurity / Congenital rubella
Continuous murmurBounding pulsesPrematurityRubellaIndomethacin closes
💊 Treatment / ECG
Indomethacin/ibuprofen (premature infants). Catheter ligation or surgical closure. PGE1 to KEEP OPEN in ductus-dependent lesions (TGA, critical coarctation). ECG: LV enlargement, LA enlargement.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
ONLY continuous (systole + diastole) murmur in common cardiology
Indomethacin CLOSES; PGE1 OPENS — two opposite uses, heavily tested
Wide pulse pressure = large SV (forward) + diastolic runoff (backward)
Congenital rubella = classic risk factor association
Bounding pulses + wide pulse pressure can also occur in AR — differentiate by murmur timing: continuous = PDA; early diastolic = AR.
TOF — Tetralogy of Fallot
PROVe mnemonic · Most common cyanotic CHD · Squatting relieves
Cyanotic
⚙ MOA / Pathophys
PROVe: Pulmonary stenosis + RV Hypertrophy + Overriding aorta + VSD. PS obstructs RV outflow → R→L shunt across VSD → deoxygenated blood enters systemic circulation → cyanosis. More PS = more R→L = more cyanosis.
🩺 Presentation / Buzzwords
Cyanotic child. Squatting relieves (↑ SVR → ↓ R→L shunt). Tet spells (crying → acute cyanosis). Boot-shaped heart CXR. DiGeorge (22q11) association.
PROVeSquatting relievesBoot heartTet spellsDiGeorge
💊 Treatment / ECG
PGE1 acutely (maintains PDA). Knee-chest position for Tet spells. Surgical repair (patch VSD + relieve RVOTO). ECG: RVH, RAD, RBBB.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
PROVe = Pulmonary stenosis, RV hypertrophy, Overriding aorta, VSD
Squatting ↑ SVR → ↓ R→L shunt → ↑ pulmonary flow → ↓ cyanosis
Most common CYANOTIC congenital heart defect
PS severity determines degree of cyanosis
TOF = progressive cyanosis (squatting, tet spells). TGA = IMMEDIATE cyanosis at birth. DiGeorge = CATCH-22: conotruncal defects + thymic aplasia + hypocalcemia.
TGA — Transposition · Coarctation of the Aorta
TGA: immediate cyanosis, maternal DM · Coarc: arm-leg BP differential, Turner syndrome
Cyanotic / Non-cyanotic
⚙ TGA MOA
Aorta arises from RV; PA from LV → parallel circulations. Survival requires mixing (PDA/ASD/VSD). PGE1 maintains PDA. Arterial switch operation is definitive fix. Maternal diabetes = risk factor.
🩺 TGA Presentation
Immediate cyanosis at birth. No murmur. O2 doesn't help. Egg-on-a-string CXR (narrow mediastinum).
Immediate cyanosisMaternal DMEgg-on-string CXRPGE1 bridge
⚙ Coarctation MOA / Presentation
Aortic narrowing → upper extremity HTN + weak femoral pulses. Rib notching (intercostal collaterals). Turner syndrome (45,X) + bicuspid AV.
Arm > leg BPRib notchingTurner 45,XBicuspid AV
⭐ High-Yield Facts
TGA: O2 does NOT help — parallel circulations, not lung disease
TGA: arterial switch must be done within first weeks (LV de-conditions)
Coarc: arm-leg BP differential is pathognomonic — always check BOTH
Coarc + Turner syndrome + bicuspid AV = classic triad
TGA = immediate cyanosis (birth). TOF = progressive cyanosis. TGA no murmur. Coarctation: associated with berry aneurysms of Circle of Willis.

❤️ Ischemia / MI

3 diseases + timeline
Angina Spectrum — Stable · Unstable · NSTEMI · STEMI · Prinzmetal
Key differentiator: troponin + ECG pattern + trigger
Ischemia
⚙ MOA / Pathophys
Stable: Fixed plaque >70% → demand ischemia with exertion only. No rupture, no troponin.
ACS (UA/NSTEMI/STEMI): Plaque RUPTURE → thrombosis. UA/NSTEMI = partial occlusion. STEMI = complete occlusion → transmural.
Prinzmetal: Coronary vasospasm → transient complete occlusion → transient ST elevation. Normal coronaries on cath.
🩺 Presentation Comparison
Stable: Exertional only, relieved by rest/NTG. Normal troponin. ST depression during pain.
UA: Rest pain or crescendo. Normal troponin. Variable ECG.
NSTEMI: Rest pain. ↑ Troponin. ST depression / T-wave changes.
STEMI: Rest pain. ↑ Troponin. ST elevation + reciprocal changes.
Prinzmetal: Rest pain (nocturnal). Usually normal troponin. Transient ST elevation → resolves.
💊 Treatment
Stable: SL NTG, BB (chronic), aspirin, statin.
ACS: Aspirin + P2Y12 + anticoagulant + statin + BB + ACEi. STEMI: urgent PCI (<90min) or thrombolytics if PCI unavailable >120min.
Prinzmetal: CCB + long-acting nitrates. AVOID beta-blockers (worsen vasospasm).
ECG: STEMI = ST elevation + RECIPROCAL changes. Pericarditis = diffuse ST + NO reciprocal + PR depression.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
Troponin = infarction. No troponin = ischemia only (stable/UA)
Prinzmetal: normal coronaries on cath + transient ST elevation. CCB treatment. AVOID BB
STEMI ECG: ST elevation + RECIPROCAL changes in opposite leads (vs pericarditis = no reciprocal)
LAD = anterior (V1–V4). RCA = inferior (II,III,aVF). LCx = lateral (I, aVL)
Stable angina does NOT cause troponin elevation — if troponin rises, it's NSTEMI. Prinzmetal: ST elevation resolves spontaneously → not STEMI. BB avoided in Prinzmetal (vasospasm worsening).
MI Histology Timeline · Post-MI Complications · Biomarkers
3–7 days = MAX rupture risk (macrophages) · EARLY = electrical · LATE = structural
MI / Path
🔬 Histology Timeline
0–4h: Wavy fibers (EM only). No gross change. Coagulative necrosis starts.
4–24h: Coagulative necrosis. Neutrophils start. Dark mottling. V-fib risk. EARLY = ELECTRICAL.
1–3d (N=Neutrophils): Neutrophils dominate. Loss of nuclei/striations. Yellow-tan center forms.
★ 3–7d (M=Macrophages): MAX RUPTURE RISK. Macrophages digest dead tissue before scar. Soft yellow-tan + red border.
7–14d: Granulation tissue. Fibroblasts + neovascularization.
2+wks: Dense collagenous scar. Gray-white firm. Aneurysm risk.
⚡ Post-MI Complications
< 24h: Arrhythmia (V-fib) — most common early cause of death
1–3d: Fibrinous pericarditis (transmural only) — pleuritic pain + rub
★ 3–7d: Free wall rupture → hemopericardium → tamponade
★ 3–7d: Papillary muscle rupture → acute MR → apex→axilla murmur
★ 3–7d: Septal rupture → acute VSD → LLSB murmur
Days–wks: Mural thrombus → embolic stroke
2–10wks: Dressler syndrome — autoimmune, negative troponin
Weeks–months: Ventricular aneurysm — persistent ST elevation
📊 Biomarkers + Arteries
Troponin I/T: Rise 3–4h · Peak 24–48h · Duration 7–10d · Best: ACUTE MI (most specific)
CK-MB: Rise 4–6h · Peak ~24h · Duration ~2d · Best: RE-INFARCTION (returns to baseline)
Myoglobin: Rise 1–2h (earliest) · Not cardiac-specific

LAD: V1–V4 (anterior) — widow-maker, LBBB risk
RCA: II, III, aVF (inferior) — AV node, RV infarct
LCx: I, aVL (lateral) — can be electrically silent
⭐ High-Yield Facts
N=Neutrophils (1–3d), M=Macrophages (3–7d) = MAX rupture window mnemonic
EARLY (<24h) = ELECTRICAL (V-fib). LATE (3–7d) = STRUCTURAL (ruptures)
All 3 ruptures (free wall, papillary, septal) occur at 3–7d window
CK-MB is BEST for reinfarction — returns to baseline in 2 days
Ventricular aneurysm (late) = persistent ST elevation ≠ new infarct
Papillary rupture (apex→axilla MR) vs Septal rupture (LLSB VSD) — both holosystolic post-MI. Location = key differentiator. Dressler (2–10wks): negative troponin = autoimmune, not reinfarction.

💚 Cardiomyopathies

3 types + special CMs
DCM · HCM · RCM — Core Comparison
EF is the first differentiator: Low (DCM) · Normal/High (HCM) · Normal (RCM)
Cardiomyopathy
⚙ MOA + Pathophys
DCM: Myocyte damage → 4-chamber dilation → ↓ EF → systolic HF. Causes: Alcohol, Coxsackie B, Doxorubicin, Peripartum, Chagas, TTN gene mutation. "Flabby heart" >500g.
HCM: Sarcomere mutation (MYH7/MYBPC3, autosomal dominant) → asymmetric septal hypertrophy → LVOTO + diastolic dysfunction. SAM of MV. Myofiber disarray on histo.
RCM: Infiltration (amyloid/iron/granuloma) → ↑ wall stiffness → impaired diastolic filling. Normal EF preserved.
🩺 Presentation + Histology
DCM: EF ≤40%. S3 gallop. Dyspnea/edema. Histo: myocyte hypertrophy + interstitial fibrosis + ninja star nuclei (TTN).
HCM: EF normal/HIGH. S4 gallop. Young athlete SCD. Asymmetric septal hypertrophy. Histo: myofiber disarray (pathognomonic).
RCM: EF ≥50%. Impaired filling. Biatrial dilation. Amyloid: Congo red → apple-green birefringence. "Granular sparkling" echo.
💊 Treatment + Dynamic Maneuver
DCM: GDMT (BB + ARNI + MRA + SGLT2i). Diuretics.
HCM: BB or verapamil. ICD for SCD risk. Septal myectomy/alcohol ablation if refractory. AVOID nitrates, diuretics, vasodilators (↓ preload → ↑ LVOTO).
RCM: Treat underlying cause. Diuretics for congestion.

Valsalva: HCM = LOUDER (↓ preload → ↑ LVOTO). All others = softer.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
HCM murmur: ONLY murmur that gets LOUDER with Valsalva/standing
Myofiber disarray = HCM pathognomonic histologic finding
Congo red → apple-green birefringence = amyloid (RCM)
RCM vs Constrictive pericarditis: CT pericardium = key — normal (RCM) vs thickened/calcified (constrictive)
DCM: alcohol reversible if stopped early. Doxorubicin: dose-dependent, NOT reversible
Low EF rules out HCM — HCM has preserved/supranormal EF. Nitrates CONTRAINDICATED in HCM (↓ preload → ↑ LVOTO). Prussian blue = iron; PAS = glycogen; Congo red = amyloid.

💜 Heart Failure

HFrEF · HFpEF · GDMT
HFrEF (Systolic) · HFpEF (Diastolic) · GDMT
EF ≤40% (HFrEF) vs EF ≥50% (HFpEF) · Normal EF ≠ normal heart
Heart Failure
⚙ MOA + Pathophys
HFrEF: ↓ contractility → ↓ EF → ↓ SV → RAAS/SNS activation → neurohormonal remodeling → progressive worsening. S3 gallop (blood sloshing into dilated LV).
HFpEF: Concentric LVH (HTN) → stiff LV → cannot relax in diastole → ↑ filling pressures → pulmonary congestion DESPITE normal EF. S4 gallop (atrial kick hits stiff LV).
Left HF = Lungs. Right HF = Rest of body.
🩺 Presentation
Left HF: Dyspnea, orthopnea, PND, crackles, displaced PMI, pink frothy sputum.
Right HF: JVD, hepatomegaly, ascites, peripheral pitting edema. Most common cause = left-sided HF → pulmonary HTN → RV overload.
BNP: Elevated in BOTH (ventricular stretch). S3 = HFrEF. S4 = HFpEF.
💊 GDMT (HFrEF — all 4 proven)
1. Beta-blocker: Carvedilol / Metoprolol succinate / Bisoprolol. ONLY when euvolemic. NEVER in acute decompensation.
2. ARNI: Sacubitril-valsartan (preferred) or ACEi/ARB. Cannot combine ARNI + ACEi.
3. MRA: Spironolactone. Hold if K+>5.0 or eGFR<30.
4. SGLT2i: Dapagliflozin / Empagliflozin. Needs eGFR>20–30.

HFpEF: BP/HR control. Diuretics for symptoms. SGLT2i emerging benefit. No GDMT with proven survival benefit yet.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
All 4 GDMT pillars have PROVEN MORTALITY BENEFIT in HFrEF
Normal EF ≠ normal heart — HFpEF patients have full HF symptoms with EF ≥50%
S3 = dilated volume-overloaded LV (HFrEF). S4 = stiff non-compliant LV (HFpEF)
Diuretics = symptom relief ONLY — no mortality benefit in either type
MRA: hold if K+>5 (hyperkalemia). Switch eplerenone if gynecomastia
Never start beta-blockers in ACUTE decompensated HF — euvolemic first. Diuretics relieve symptoms but don't improve survival. ARNI cannot be combined with ACEi (36h washout needed — angioedema risk).

🟡 Valvular Disease

AS · AR · MR · MS · MVP
Aortic Stenosis · Aortic Regurgitation · Mitral Regurgitation · Mitral Stenosis · MVP
Murmur location + radiation + LV response + dynamic maneuvers
Valvular
⚙ MOA + Pathophys
AS: Outflow obstruction → pressure overload → concentric LVH. SAD triad (Syncope/Angina/Dyspnea). Elderly: calcific. Young: bicuspid AV.
AR: Diastolic backflow → volume overload → eccentric LVH. Wide pulse pressure. Marfan/endocarditis. Bounding pulses. de Musset sign.
MR: Systolic backflow → LA + LV volume overload → eccentric LVH. Papillary rupture post-MI = acute MR.
MS: LA outflow obstruction → ↑ LA pressure → pulmonary HTN. No LVH (LV protected). Rheumatic fever = classic cause.
MVP: Myxomatous degeneration → leaflet prolapse into LA during systole.
🩺 Murmur + Key Features
AS: Crescendo-decrescendo systolic at RUSB → carotids. Pulsus parvus et tardus.
AR: Early diastolic decrescendo at LSB (lean forward). Wide pulse pressure.
MR: Holosystolic at apex → axilla. ↑ with handgrip.
MS: Diastolic rumble at apex + opening snap. OS closer to S2 = MORE severe.
MVP: Mid-systolic click ± late systolic murmur. Valsalva → click EARLIER.
💊 Treatment + Dynamic Maneuvers
AS: SAVR/TAVR when severe + symptomatic.
AR: Vasodilators (nifedipine). Surgery when EF<50% or LVESD>50mm.
MR: Vasodilators (ACEi). Surgery when symptomatic or LV decompensation.
MS: HR control (BB/CCB). PBMV if anatomy favorable. Anticoag if Afib.
MVP: Usually none; BB for sx.

Valsalva: All murmurs softer EXCEPT HCM (louder) and MVP click (earlier).
Handgrip: MR/AR louder; HCM/AS softer.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
AS: SAD triad. RUSB → carotids. Concentric LVH (pressure overload)
AR: wide pulse pressure + bounding pulses. Early diastolic at LSB leaning forward
MR: holosystolic apex → axilla. Papillary rupture at 3–7d post-MI
MS: OS + diastolic rumble. OS closer to S2 = MORE severe. LA enlarges → Afib. No LVH
MVP: mid-systolic click. Valsalva → click moves EARLIER (↓ LV volume → earlier prolapse)
AS vs HCM: both systolic ejection murmurs — AS = RUSB/carotids/no Valsalva change. HCM = LUSB/Valsalva LOUDER. MR vs VSD: both holosystolic — MR = apex→axilla; VSD = LLSB. MS: OS CLOSER to S2 = MORE severe (counterintuitive).

🔵 Pericardial Disease

Pericarditis · Tamponade · Constrictive · Dressler
Acute Pericarditis · Tamponade · Constrictive · Dressler Syndrome
Constrictive vs RCM = CT pericardium · Tamponade = Beck's triad · PR depression = pericarditis
Pericardial
⚙ MOA + Pathophys
Pericarditis: Pericardial inflammation → friction rub. ECG: diffuse ST elevation + PR depression (pathognomonic) + NO reciprocal changes. Viral (Coxsackie B) = #1 cause.
Tamponade: Fluid accumulates → ↑ intrapericardial pressure → compresses all chambers → ↓ CO → obstructive shock. Electrical alternans (swinging heart).
Constrictive: Fibrosis/calcification → rigid shell → impairs late diastolic filling. Diastolic equalization all chambers.
Dressler: Autoimmune pericarditis 2–10 weeks post-MI. Anti-heart antibodies. Negative troponin.
🩺 Presentation + Buzzwords
Pericarditis: Sharp pleuritic pain. Worse lying, better leaning forward. Friction rub.
Tamponade: Beck's Triad: Hypotension + JVD + Muffled heart sounds. Pulsus paradoxus >10mmHg. Water-bottle CXR.
Constrictive: Kussmaul sign (JVP RISES with inspiration). Edema, ascites. Pericardial calcification on CT.
Dressler: Fever + pleuritic pain + rub at 2–10 WEEKS post-MI. Negative troponin.
💊 Treatment
Pericarditis: NSAIDs + colchicine. No steroids for first episode.
Tamponade: Urgent pericardiocentesis. IV fluids temporizing. Avoid diuretics/vasodilators (preload-dependent).
Constrictive: Pericardiectomy (surgically curable).
Dressler: NSAIDs (aspirin preferred post-MI). Colchicine.

Constrictive vs RCM: CT pericardium thickened = constrictive (curable). Normal = RCM (not curable).
⭐ High-Yield Facts
PR depression = pathognomonic for pericarditis (not seen in STEMI)
STEMI vs pericarditis: STEMI has RECIPROCAL changes; pericarditis has diffuse ST + PR depression + NO reciprocal
Beck's triad: hypotension + JVD + muffled sounds = tamponade emergency
Kussmaul sign: JVP RISES with inspiration (opposite of normal) = constrictive pericarditis
Dressler: 2–10 weeks post-MI, NEGATIVE troponin = autoimmune (not reinfarction)
JVD + hypotension post-MI → tamponade (free wall rupture), NOT HF. Do NOT give diuretics in tamponade. Steroids worsen recurrent pericarditis — avoid first episode. Constrictive is SURGICALLY CURABLE — don't miss it.

🟠 Vascular Disease & Vasculitis

Dissection · Aneurysms · GCA · Kawasaki · PAN · GPA · Vasculitis spectrum
Aortic Dissection · Aneurysms · Vasculitis by Vessel Size
Type A = surgical · Type B = labetalol · GCA = steroids before biopsy · Kawasaki = IVIG + aspirin
Vascular
⚙ MOA + Vascular Diseases
Aortic Dissection: Intimal tear → false lumen. Type A (ascending) = surgical emergency. Type B (descending) = labetalol (↓ HR + ↓ BP). Marfan/HTN = risk.
AAA: Atherosclerosis → medial weakness. Infrarenal. Smoking/male/>65. Repair >5cm.
Syphilitic aortitis: Vasa vasorum endarteritis → ascending aorta aneurysm → AR. Tree-bark appearance. Tertiary syphilis.
Berry aneurysm: Circle of Willis bifurcations. ADPKD. "Worst headache of life" = subarachnoid.
Charcot-Bouchard: Chronic HTN → deep brain microaneurysm → hemorrhage (basal ganglia).
🩺 Vasculitis by Vessel Size
LARGE: GCA (elderly woman >50, jaw claudication, vision loss, ↑ESR, skip lesions). Takayasu (young Asian woman, pulseless arms).
MEDIUM: Kawasaki (child <5, CRASH, coronary aneurysm, IVIG+aspirin). PAN (HepB, ANCA neg, string of beads, spares lungs). Buerger (young male smoker, distal ulcers, stop smoking only Tx).
SMALL (ANCA): GPA/Wegener (c-ANCA/PR3, sinuses+lungs+renal, saddle nose). MPA (p-ANCA/MPO, pulmonary-renal, no upper resp/granulomas). EGPA (p-ANCA, asthma+eosinophilia).
SMALL (immune): HSP/IgA vasculitis (child, palpable purpura buttocks, IgA deposits, URI trigger).
💊 Treatment + Key Tests
Dissection Type A: Emergent surgery.
Dissection Type B: IV labetalol (target BP<120, HR<60).
GCA: HIGH-DOSE STEROIDS IMMEDIATELY (before biopsy). Temporal artery biopsy confirms.
Kawasaki: IVIG + high-dose aspirin (anti-inflam) → then low-dose aspirin (antiplatelet).
PAN: Steroids ± cyclophosphamide + antivirals if HepB+.
GPA: Cyclophosphamide + steroids. Monitor c-ANCA for relapse.
Buerger: STOP SMOKING — only treatment.
Fibromuscular dysplasia: Young woman, renal HTN, string of pearls angiography.
⭐ High-Yield Facts
GCA: start steroids BEFORE biopsy — vision loss risk too high to wait
Kawasaki: #1 acquired heart disease in children. CRASH criteria. Coronary aneurysm on echo
PAN: ANCA NEGATIVE + spares lungs/glomeruli + Hep B association
c-ANCA (PR3) = GPA (Wegener). p-ANCA (MPO) = MPA / EGPA. Know cold.
Type A dissection: can rupture into pericardium → tamponade. Labetalol ↓ dP/dt (HR + BP)
Do NOT use pure vasodilator alone in dissection (reflex tachycardia worsens). Labetalol first. Thrombolytics in dissection = FATAL (worsens). GCA skip lesions: biopsy must be large (>2cm). Kawasaki: aspirin OK here despite Reye risk — specific exception.

🩷 Infective Endocarditis

Organism map · Skin findings · Sterile vegetations
Infective Endocarditis — Organism Map · Skin Findings · Sterile Vegetations
Janeway = painless · Osler = painful · S. bovis = colorectal cancer · Libman-Sacks = SLE
Endocarditis
⚙ Organism by Context
S. aureus: IVDU / normal valves → ACUTE, destructive, large vegetations, rapid
S. viridans: Dental procedure / damaged valves → SUBACUTE, smaller vegetations
S. bovis (S. gallolyticus): Any valve → COLORECTAL CANCER (always order colonoscopy)
S. epidermidis: Prosthetic valve (early <60d) → skin contamination at surgery
Enterococcus: GU or GI procedure → any valve
HACEK: Fastidious gram-negatives → culture-negative IE
🩺 Presentation + Skin Findings
Fever + new/changed murmur + systemic signs. Duke criteria (major: positive blood cultures + echo evidence; minor: fever, risk factors, vascular phenomena).

Janeway: PAINLESS macules (palms/soles) = septic microemboli
Osler nodes: PAINFUL nodules (finger pads) = immune complex deposition
Roth spots: Retinal hemorrhage + pale center = septic emboli to retina
Splinter hemorrhages: Under nails = septic emboli
💊 Treatment + Sterile Vegetations
IV antibiotics 4–6 weeks. Surgery for: severe valve dysfunction, persistent bacteremia, perivalvular extension, large vegetation/embolic risk.

Sterile vegetations (non-infectious):
• Libman-Sacks (SLE): BOTH sides of mitral valve. Sterile immune complex. LE cells.
• NBTE (Marantic): Lines of valve closure. Malignancy/DIC/hypercoag.
• Rheumatic HD: Small vegetations along closure (GAS → anti-M antibody → mitral)
⭐ High-Yield Facts
Janeway = J = Just painless (palms/soles) = SEPTIC emboli
Osler = O = Ouch = painful (finger pads) = IMMUNE COMPLEX
S. bovis bacteremia = ALWAYS colonoscopy (colorectal cancer)
Libman-Sacks = BOTH sides of mitral valve — unique to SLE (only sterile vegetation on both sides)
Tricuspid valve IE = IV drug user (right-sided, septic pulmonary emboli)
Janeway vs Osler: J = painless; O = painful. S. bovis = colon cancer — never miss. Rheumatic fever (GAS) = immune-mediated valve damage (not infection). Libman-Sacks on BOTH sides = unique SLE finding. Prophylaxis only for high-risk patients + high-risk procedures.
