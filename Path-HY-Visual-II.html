Dilated (DCM)

Gross4-chamber dilation; "flabby heart" >500g

GeneTTN (titin) — most common genetic

Hypertrophic (HCM)

GrossAsymmetric septal hypertrophy; "banana" LV; LVOTO
HistologyMyofiber disarray + interstitial fibrosis ★

Restrictive (RCM)

EFNormal ≥ 50% — compliance ↓
GrossNormal wall thickness; biatrial dilation
HistologyCongo red → apple-green birefringence (amyloid) ★
GeneTTR (transthyretin) amyloid

Vs const.Normal pericardium on CT (vs constrictive = thickened)
⚠️ KEY TRAP: HCM murmur LOUDER with Valsalva/standing (↓ preload → ↑ LVOTO). All other murmurs get softer. Also avoid nitrates/diuretics in HCM — they worsen obstruction. Low EF rules out HCM.

L→R vs R→L Shunts — The Core Framework

Left → Right Shunts Acyanotic

Blood flows from high-pressure left side to right
↑ Pulmonary blood flow → RV volume overload
Acyanotic initially — oxygenated blood enters right heart
Can cause Eisenmenger syndrome (late cyanosis) if untreated
Examples: VSD, ASD, PDA

Right → Left Shunts Cyanotic

Deoxygenated blood enters systemic circulation
Cyanosis from birth or early infancy
O₂ supplementation does NOT correct cyanosis
Risk of brain abscess from paradoxical emboli (bypass lung filter)
Examples: TOF, TGA, truncus arteriosus, TAPVR

Eisenmenger Syndrome — The Shunt Reversal

Long-standing L→R shunt → Pulmonary HTN → Shunt reversal → Late cyanosis

Untreated L→R shunt → ↑ pulmonary blood flow
→ Pulmonary HTN develops over years
→ RV pressure rises until it equals LV pressure
→ Shunt reverses to R→L → late-onset cyanosis + clubbing
Surgical closure is CONTRAINDICATED once Eisenmenger is established. The VSD/ASD/PDA is now acting as a "pop-off valve" — closing it leaves the RV with no escape from high pressure → acute RV failure and death.

Valve-by-Valve Deep Dive

Aortic Stenosis (AS)

SAD triadSyncope (~3yr) → Angina (~5yr) → Dyspnea/HF (~1–2yr)
CausesBicuspid AV (young); senile calcification of fibrosa layer (elderly)
PulsePulsus parvus et tardus (weak + delayed carotid upstroke)
LVConcentric LVH (pressure overload)
PathologyCalcification starts in fibrosa layer (dense collagen, outflow side)

Aortic Regurgitation (AR)

SignsWater-hammer pulse; de Musset (head bob); Quincke (nail pulsations); Hill sign
Pulse pressureWide (↑ SBP from ↑ SV; ↓ DBP from regurgitation)
LVEccentric LVH (volume overload)
CausesMarfan, aortic root dilation, bicuspid AV, endocarditis, syphilitic aortitis
Acute ARSurgical emergency — no time for eccentric remodeling

Mitral Stenosis (MS)

Opening snapOS closer to S2 = MORE severe (higher LA pressure opens valve sooner)
LVNO LVH — LV is underfilled. LA enlarges instead → AFib
CascadeMS → ↑ LA pressure → LA dilation → AFib → pulm HTN → RHF
CauseRheumatic fever → commissural fusion → "fish-mouth" valve
PathologyOnly significant valve lesion that does NOT cause LVH

MVP (Mitral Valve Prolapse)

Click timingValsalva/Standing → click EARLIER (↓ LV vol → prolapse sooner)
SquattingClick moves LATER (↑ LV volume → prolapse delayed)
