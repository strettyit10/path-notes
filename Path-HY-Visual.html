HFrEF vs HFpEF

HFrEF — Systolic Failure

EF≤ 40%
ProblemLV can't squeeze → ↓ stroke volume
CausesDCM, post-MI, MR, AR, alcohol, viral
GallopS3 — dilated LV, early diastole
SymptomCrackles, orthopnea, displaced PMI
TreatmentGDMT: BB + ARNI + MRA + SGLT2i

HFpEF — Diastolic Failure

EF≥ 50%
ProblemLV too stiff to fill → ↑ filling pressures
CausesHTN, HCM, amyloid, concentric LVH
GallopS4 — stiff LV, atrial kick, late diastole
SymptomSame congestion symptoms as HFrEF
TreatmentBP/HR control; diuretics for congestion
⚠️ TRAP: Normal EF ≠ normal heart. HFpEF patients have full-blown HF symptoms with EF ≥50%. Diuretics relieve symptoms in both types but provide no mortality benefit in either.
GDMT — 4 Pillars for HFrEF

Beta-Blocker

Carvedilol · Metoprolol succinate · Bisoprolol
Only when euvolemic — never in acute decompensation

ARNI / ACEi / ARB

Sacubitril-valsartan preferred · Blocks RAAS remodeling
Reduces hospitalizations and mortality

MRA (Spironolactone)

Hold if K+ >5.0 or eGFR <30
Switch to eplerenone for gynecomastia

SGLT2 Inhibitor

Dapagliflozin · Empagliflozin
Requires eGFR >20–30. Also benefits CKD and T2DM

Four Pericardial Conditions — Side by Side

Acute Pericarditis

Sharp pain, worse lying flat, better leaning forward
Friction rub (3-component)
ECG: diffuse ST elevation + PR depression + no reciprocal changes
Most common cause: viral (Coxsackie B)
Rx: NSAIDs + colchicine

Cardiac Tamponade

Beck's Triad: Hypotension + JVD + Muffled sounds
Pulsus paradoxus >10 mmHg
Electrical alternans on ECG
"Water-bottle" CXR silhouette
Rx: Urgent pericardiocentesis

Constrictive Pericarditis

Kussmaul sign: JVP rises with inspiration
Pericardial calcification on CT/MRI
"Square root sign" on cardiac cath
TB, radiation, prior surgery are causes
Rx: Pericardiectomy (surgically curable)

Dressler Syndrome

Onset 2–10 weeks post-MI
Fever + pleuritic chest pain + friction rub
Negative troponin (not reinfarction)
Anti-heart antibodies (autoimmune)
Rx: NSAIDs
Constrictive Pericarditis vs Restrictive Cardiomyopathy
FeatureConstrictive PericarditisRestrictive Cardiomyopathy
Pericardium on CT/MRIThickened / calcifiedNormal
Kussmaul signPresent (JVP ↑ with inspiration)Variable / less reliable
Cath finding"Square root sign" — all chambers equalizeSimilar dip and plateau pattern
TreatmentPericardiectomy (surgically curable)Treat underlying cause — not surgically curable

⚠️ KEY RULE: Pericardial thickening on CT → constrictive (surgically curable). Normal pericardium on CT → restrictive CMP (myocardial disease). This one imaging finding determines the diagnosis and management.
Aortic Dissection — Type A vs Type B

Type A — Ascending Aorta

SURGICAL EMERGENCY
LocationAscending aorta (proximal to L subclavian)
RisksRupture into pericardium → tamponade; AR from root distortion; MI from coronary ostia involvement
ManagementEmergent surgical repair
CausesChronic HTN (most common); Marfan/EDS (young patient); bicuspid AV

Type B — Descending Aorta

MEDICAL MANAGEMENT
LocationDescending aorta (distal to L subclavian)
RisksMesenteric or renal ischemia if propagates; limb ischemia
ManagementLabetalol — ↓ BP + ↓ HR to reduce aortic wall stress
CausesChronic HTN; atherosclerosis
⚠️ Presentation rule: Tearing/ripping chest pain radiating to the back + arm BP differential (>20 mmHg) = dissection until proven otherwise. Order CT angiography — not echo (can miss), not troponin-first workup.
Aneurysm Comparison — Location is the Key

AAA

Location: Infrarenal abdominal aorta
Cause: Atherosclerosis
Patient: Elderly male smoker
Clue: Pulsatile abdominal mass
Threshold: Repair at >5 cm

Syphilitic Aortitis

Location: Ascending aorta (always)
Cause: Tertiary syphilis → vasa vasorum endarteritis
Clue: "Tree-bark" intima; AR from root dilation
Pathology: Elastic fiber destruction

Berry Aneurysm

Location: Circle of Willis bifurcations
Cause: Congenital media defect
Associations: ADPKD, coarctation
Presentation: SAH — "worst headache of life" + meningismus

Charcot-Bouchard

Location: Deep brain arterioles (basal ganglia, thalamus)
Cause: Chronic HTN → lipohyalinosis
Trigger: Cocaine (acute BP spike)
Presentation: Deep intracerebral hemorrhage

Small Vessel — ANCA Associated
c-ANCA (PR3)

GPA (Wegener)

Upper + lower respiratory + GN · saddle-nose deformity · cavitary nodules · RBC casts
Necrotizing granulomatous · Treat with cyclophosphamide + steroids
p-ANCA (MPO)

MPA (Microscopic Polyangiitis)

Pulmonary-renal syndrome · No upper respiratory · No granulomas
Distinguishable from GPA by absence of upper respiratory/granulomas
p-ANCA (MPO)

EGPA (Churg-Strauss)

Adult-onset asthma + eosinophilia + vasculitis
Asthma is the sentinel feature. ↑ eosinophils. Steroid-dependent asthma = warning sign.
