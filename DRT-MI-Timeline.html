0 – 4 HOURS
Early / No Changes
Normal or near-normal on light microscopy

HISTOLOGY

Wavy myofibers (early EM finding). Edema begins. Coagulative necrosis just starting.

GROSS

No gross change visible. Heart appears normal to eye.

BIOMARKERS

Troponin starts rising ~3–4 hours after onset. CK-MB rises 4–6 hours. Nothing may be elevated at 0–4h.

4 – 24 HOURS
Coagulative Necrosis Begins
Neutrophils start; arrhythmia risk highest ⚡
⚡ HIGHEST ARRHYTHMIA RISK

HISTOLOGY

Coagulative necrosis: proteins denature → cell outlines preserved (ghosting). Neutrophil margination begins. Karyolysis/pyknosis starting.

GROSS

Dark mottling begins. Heart muscle appears darker/dusky in affected area.

CLINICAL KEY

Most common early cause of death = ventricular fibrillation. EARLY = ELECTRICAL. Troponin peaks at 24–48h.

1 – 3 DAYS
Neutrophil Infiltration (Peak)
N for Neutrophils

HISTOLOGY

Neutrophils dominate. Loss of nuclei and striations from necrotic myocytes. Ongoing coagulative necrosis.

GROSS

Mottled yellow-tan center beginning to form. Hyperemic (red) border from inflammatory response at margins.

EXAM NOTE

Early rupture possible but not peak risk. Fibrinous pericarditis can appear (transmural infarct → pericardial inflammation).

3 – 7 DAYS ★ MOST TESTED
Macrophage Infiltration — MAX Rupture Risk
M for Macrophages. Yellow-tan softening + red border.
⚠️ HIGHEST STRUCTURAL RUPTURE RISK

HISTOLOGY

Macrophages dominate. Phagocytosis of necrotic debris. Macrophages release MMPs → digest dead tissue. Wall at maximum weakness.

GROSS

Soft yellow-tan center + bright red hyperemic border. Classic NBME gross appearance question.

COMPLICATIONS AT THIS WINDOW

• Free wall rupture → hemopericardium → cardiac tamponade → sudden death
• Papillary muscle rupture → acute severe MR → flash pulmonary edema
• Septal rupture → acute VSD → new LLSB holosystolic murmur
All occur because macrophages digest dead tissue before scar has formed.

7 – 14 DAYS
Granulation Tissue Forms
Healing begins. Neovascularization.

HISTOLOGY

Granulation tissue at margins. Fibroblasts + new capillaries (neovascularization). Inflammation subsiding.

GROSS

Red-gray depressed infarct borders. Yellow-tan center persisting but firmness returning.

2 + WEEKS → MONTHS
Dense Collagenous Scar (Complete Healing)
Gray-white firm scar. Ventricular aneurysm risk.

HISTOLOGY

Dense collagen replaces necrotic tissue. Scar is stronger than granulation tissue but less compliant than normal myocardium.

GROSS

Gray-white firm scar. Depressed compared to surrounding myocardium.

LATE COMPLICATIONS

Ventricular aneurysm (scarred wall balloons outward → persistent ST elevation + mural thrombus risk → embolic stroke). Dressler syndrome (2–10 weeks: autoimmune pericarditis).

CORONARY ARTERY TERRITORIES

LAD
V1 – V4
Anterior wall + anterior septum. Most common culprit. "Widow-maker." LBBB risk. LV systolic failure.
RCA
II, III, aVF
Inferior wall + RV. Supplies AV node (~85% of people). Inferior MI → watch for AV block. RV infarct.
LCx
I, aVL
Lateral wall of LV. Can be "electrically silent." Lateral MI may have subtle ECG changes.

POST-MI COMPLICATION SUMMARY

COMPLICATION TIMING KEY CLUE
Arrhythmia (V-fib) <24 hours Most common early cause of death. EARLY = ELECTRICAL.
Fibrinous pericarditis 1–3 days Transmural infarct → pericardial inflammation. Pleuritic pain + friction rub.
Free wall rupture (LV) 3–7 days ★ Cardiac tamponade → sudden death. Hemopericardium at autopsy.
Papillary muscle rupture 3–7 days ★ Acute MR → flash pulmonary edema. Murmur at apex → axilla.
Septal rupture (VSD) 3–7 days ★ New harsh holosystolic murmur at LLSB. Acute HF.
Mural thrombus Days–weeks Stasis over akinetic LV wall → thrombus → embolic stroke.
Dressler syndrome 2–10 weeks Autoimmune pericarditis. Anti-heart antibodies. Fever + pleuritic pain.
Ventricular aneurysm Weeks–months Persistent ST elevation. HF + arrhythmias. Mural thrombus risk.
